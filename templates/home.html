Empowering Women

Taking Control of Your Breast Health

Early detection and prevention are your strongest allies in the fight against breast cancer. Here's how you can take proactive steps:

  • Regular Self-Exams - Get familiar with how your breasts normally look and feel so you can notice any changes.
  • Annual Mammograms - Women over 40 should schedule yearly mammograms for early detection.
  • Healthy Lifestyle - Maintain a healthy weight, exercise regularly, and limit alcohol consumption.
  • Know Your Family History - Share your family health history with your doctor to assess your risk.
  • Breastfeed If Possible - Breastfeeding can lower breast cancer risk, especially when continued for a year or more.
  • Avoid Hormone Therapy - Limit post-menopausal hormone therapy as it can increase risk.
Did You Know?

When detected early, the 5-year survival rate for breast cancer is over 90%. Regular screenings and awareness are key to early detection.
